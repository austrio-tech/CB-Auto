## Driving in Oman — General Facts

- Oman drives on the **RIGHT** side of the road.
- Speed limits: **120 km/h** on highways, **80 km/h** outside cities, **60 km/h** inside cities, **40 km/h** in school zones.
- Omani driving conditions are harsh on vehicles: extreme heat, sand, dust, and long distances between cities.
- Most Omanis own large 4x4 or SUV vehicles due to terrain (mountains, wadis, desert dunes).
- Petrol and diesel are heavily subsidised in Oman, making large-engine vehicles practical to run.

## Climate Impact on Vehicle Parts — Oman Specific

### Extreme Heat (Summer — June to September)

Air temperatures 40–50°C; engine bay temperatures can reach 80–100°C.

| Part | Impact |
|------|--------|
| **Battery** | Accelerated electrolyte evaporation. Battery life: 2–3 years (vs 4–5 in Europe). |
| **Coolant** | Overheating risk if not maintained. Flush every 2 years. |
| **Drive belts** | Rubber degrades faster. Inspect every 20,000 km. |
| **Tyres** | Risk of blowout if underinflated. Check pressure monthly. |
| **Brake fluid** | Absorbs moisture faster under heat cycling. Change every 2 years (DOT4 minimum). |
| **A/C Cabin Filter** | Clogged filters reduce A/C efficiency — replace every 15,000 km. |

### Dust and Sand

| Part | Impact |
|------|--------|
| **Air filters** | Clog faster than in non-desert climates. Inspect every 10,000 km; replace by 15,000–20,000 km. |
| **Engine oil** | Contaminated faster. Change every 5,000 km (conventional), 8,000–10,000 km (synthetic). |
| **Wiper blades** | Grit degrades wiper rubber. Inspect after dust storms. |
| **Headlights** | Sand pitting reduces output over time. |
| **Body seals** | Sand erosion causes premature wear. |

### Khareef Season (Salalah, July–September)

Monsoon rains unique to Dhofar Governorate in the Arabian Peninsula.

| Part | Impact |
|------|--------|
| **Wiper blades** | Must be in excellent condition before July. |
| **Brakes** | First rain after months of dry driving — performance changes on leaf-covered mountain roads. |
| **Brake discs** | Unusual moisture can cause surface rust in just days if the car is parked unused. |
| **Tyres** | Wet grip becomes important — check tread depth. |

### Wadi Crossings and Off-Road Use

Common activity across Oman (Wadi Shab, Wadi Bani Khalid, Wadi Dayqah, Wahiba Sands).

| Part | Impact |
|------|--------|
| **Shock absorbers** | High stress, inspect every 20,000 km in off-road vehicles. |
| **Suspension bushings** | Premature wear from impacts and water ingress. |
| **Brake discs** | Warping from sudden cooling during wadi crossings (hot brakes, cold water). |
| **CV joints** | Stress from extreme angles during off-road driving. |
| **Air filter** | Critical during dusty off-road driving — check after every major off-road trip. |

## Vehicle Import and Registration in Oman

### Common Imported Vehicles

Most vehicles in Oman are imported from Japan (new and used), UAE, and Saudi Arabia.

**Popular right-hand-drive (RHD) Japanese imports (pre-2010 used):**
- Toyota Land Cruiser Prado J90/J120
- Nissan Patrol Y61 (non-Safari)
- Toyota Hilux Surf (4Runner equivalent)

> RHD vehicles use the same parts as LHD versions for most mechanical components.

### GCC Specification Vehicles

Most new cars in Oman are GCC-spec (Gulf Cooperation Council specification):
- Heavier-duty cooling systems
- Heat-resistant cable insulation
- Higher capacity air conditioning
- Different emissions standards than EU/US specs

Parts from our catalogue are compatible with GCC-spec vehicles unless otherwise noted.

## Common Roadside Breakdown Causes in Oman

Ranked by frequency based on data from our branches:

| Rank | Cause |
|------|-------|
| 1 | Flat tyre / tyre blowout (heat + under-inflation) |
| 2 | Battery failure (heat degradation) |
| 3 | Engine overheating (coolant leak, water pump failure, thermostat failure) |
| 4 | Brake fade or squealing (worn pads + heat) |
| 5 | A/C failure (compressor, refrigerant leak, cabin filter blockage) |
| 6 | Alternator failure (battery not charging) |
| 7 | Drive belt failure (V-belt, serpentine belt snap) |
| 8 | Fuel pump failure (contaminated fuel, heat) |
| 9 | Sensor fault (O2, MAF, crankshaft — common in older vehicles) |
| 10 | Suspension damage (wadi crossings, speed bumps at speed) |

### Pre-Trip Checklist for Long Drives

- ✔ Engine oil level
- ✔ Coolant level
- ✔ Tyre pressure (including spare)
- ✔ Battery condition
- ✔ Brake fluid level
- ✔ Wiper condition
- ✔ All lights working
- ✔ Fuel (plan petrol stations in advance on remote routes)

| Cracked windscreen | OMR 10 |
